Why President Lincoln is the Greatest President

Abraham Lincoln led our country through its bloodiest war and its greatest moral, constitutional, and political crisis. He preserved the Union, abolished slavery, strengthened the federal government, and modernized the U.S. economy. Lincoln managed in the process of saving the Union and abolishing slavery to define a more perfect Union in terms of liberty, justice, and economic equality that rallied the nation behind him.

Here's a timeline of President Lincoln's life

  • Feb 1809 - Abraham Lincoln is born
  • Dec 1816 - Abraham Lincoln's family settled in Indiana
  • 1831 - Lincoln worked for himself
  • Nov 1842 - Abraham Lincoln Married
  • 1856 - Lincoln helps organize the new Republican Party of Illinois
  • Mar 1861 - Inaugurated President
  • Apr 1861 - Fort Sumter attacked by rebel forces in Charleston
  • Jan 1863 - Lincoln issues the final Emancipation Proclaimed
  • Nov 1864 - Abraham Lincoln Reelected President
  • Mar 1865 - Inaugurated President for the second term
  • Apr 1865 - President Lincoln is assassinated
  • May 1922 - Lincoln Memorial first opened to the public
